Visual language: an editorial illustration made with layered gouache and transparent watercolor on warm, lightly textured paper. Use simplified confident shapes, visible brush variation, softly dissolved edges, and selective fine detail rather than uniform rendering.

Palette: warm ivory paper (#FEF9ED), muted sage green (#568259), dusty slate blue (#6B7FA3), terracotta (#B07A65), and restrained golden ochre (#C49A6C), supported by natural earth tones. Keep saturation moderate and shadows soft.

Mood: calm, thoughtful, technically serious, quietly optimistic, and handmade. Favor atmospheric natural light, gentle depth, and the visual restraint of a well-designed mid-century editorial or travel illustration.

Avoid: visible words, letters, numbers, logos, watermarks, interface elements, glossy 3D rendering, synthetic neon lighting, hyper-detailed photorealism, generic stock-photo composition, hard digital gradients, and stereotypical AI-art ornamentation.
